5 Variables | Notation
6 ------------- | -------------
7 `u,v` | (*) D-grid winds
8 ` w` | (*) Explicit vertical velocity
9  &delta; p<sup>*</sup> | (*) Layer hydrostatic pressure thickness, proportional to mass
10  &Theta; <sub>v</sub> | (*) Virtual potential temperature
11  &delta; <sub>z</sub> | (*) Layer geometric depth
12 &rho; | Total (air and all water species) mass density, equal to -&delta;p<sup>*</sup>/g&delta;z
13  Q<sub>i</sub> | (*) Density of tracer i (also written Q as a generic tracer density)
14 q<sub>i</sub> | Mixing ratio of tracer i, defined with respect to total air mass; equal to Q<sub>i</sub>/&delta;p<sup>*</sup>
15 q<sub>v</sub> | Mixing ratio of water vapor
16 p | Total cell-mean pressure
17 p<sup>*</sup> | Cell-mean hydrostatic pressure
18 p' | Cell-mean nonhydrostatic pressure component, equal to p - p<sup>*</sup>
19 &Delta;A, &Delta;x, &Delta;y | D-grid cell areas and cell face lengths
20 &Delta;A<sub>c</sub>, &Delta;x<sub>c</sub>, &Delta;y<sub>c</sub> | Dual-grid cell areas and cell face lengths
21  &Delta;t | Lagrangian dynamics (or acoustic) time step
22 &Delta;T | Vertical remapping interval
23 &Delta;&tau; | Physics time step
24 c<sub>pd</sub> | Specific heat of dry air at constant pressure
25 R<sub>d</sub> | Gas constant for dry air
26 c<sub>p</sub> | Variable specific heat of moist air
27 &kappa; | R/c<sub>p</sub>, where R is the (variable) gas constant of moist air
28 i, j, k | Spatial grid-cell indices for the local x-, y-, and z-directions, respectively, given as subscripts
29 n | time index
30 m | tracer index, given as a subscript
31 n | time index, given as a superscript: t<sup>n</sup> = n&Delta;t
32 
33 Here, a (*) indicates a prognostic variable. All variables are cell-means (or face-means for horizontal winds) unless otherwise noted. The differencing notation used in this document follows that of LR96, LR97, and L04, in which the operator &delta;<sub>x</sub> &phi; is defined as a centered-difference operator:
34 
35 \f[
36 (\delta_x \phi)_{i+1/2} = \phi_{i+1} - \phi_i \tag {B.1}
37  \f]
38 
39  The indices on dependent variables are suppressed unless explicitly needed.) This definition differs from the similar operators of in the literature intended to be second-order discretizations of a derivative; to do this with our definition of &delta;<sub>x</sub> a 1/&Delta;<sub>x</sub> term would be needed to complete the discrete derivative.

43 ##B.1 Important Relations
44 
45 Cell-mean pressure:
46 \f[
47 p^* = \frac{\delta p^*}{\delta \log p^*} \; \mathrm{(hydrostatic)} \\. \tag {B.2}
48  \f]
49 
50 \f[
51 p = \rho R_d T_v = - \frac{R_d}{g} \frac{\delta p^* T_v}{\delta z} \mathrm{(nonhydrostatic)} \tag {B.3}
52  \f]
53 
54 Conversion from temperature *T* to virtual potential temperature &Theta;<sub>v</sub> :
55 
56 \f[
57 T_v = T \left ( 1 + \epsilon q_v \right ) \tag {B.4}
58  \f]
59 
60 \f[
61 \Theta_v = T_v / p^\kappa \tag {B.5}
62  \f]
63 where &epsilon; = 1 + R<sub>v</sub>/R<sub>d</sub> and &kappa; = R/c<sub>p</sub>. Note that we do *not* include the arbitrary constant factor p<sub>0</sub><sup>&kappa;</sup> in our definition of potential temperature; our form is the equivalent to setting p<sub>0</sub> = 1Pa but simpler and more computationally efficient.
