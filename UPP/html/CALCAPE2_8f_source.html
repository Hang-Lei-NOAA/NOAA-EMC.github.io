8 ! SUBPROGRAM: CALCAPE COMPUTES CAPE AND CINS
9 ! PRGRMMR: TREADON ORG: W/NP2 DATE: 93-02-10
10 !
11 ! ABSTRACT:
12 !
13 ! THIS ROUTINE COMPUTES CAPE AND CINS GIVEN TEMPERATURE,
14 ! PRESSURE, AND SPECIFIC HUMIDTY. IN "STORM AND CLOUD
15 ! DYNAMICS" (1989, ACADEMIC PRESS) COTTON AND ANTHES DEFINE
16 ! CAPE (EQUATION 9.16, P501) AS
17 !
18 ! EL
19 ! CAPE = SUM G * LN(THETAP/THETAA) DZ
20 ! LCL
21 !
22 ! WHERE,
23 ! EL = EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL,
24 ! LCL = LIFTING CONDENSTATION LEVEL,
25 ! G = GRAVITATIONAL ACCELERATION,
26 ! THETAP = LIFTED PARCEL POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE,
27 ! THETAA = AMBIENT POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE.
28 !
29 ! NOTE THAT THE INTEGRAND LN(THETAP/THETAA) APPROXIMATELY
30 ! EQUALS (THETAP-THETAA)/THETAA. THIS RATIO IS OFTEN USED
31 ! IN THE DEFINITION OF CAPE/CINS.
32 !
33 ! TWO TYPES OF CAPE/CINS CAN BE COMPUTED BY THIS ROUTINE. THE
34 ! SUMMATION PROCESS IS THE SAME FOR BOTH CASES. WHAT DIFFERS
35 ! IS THE DEFINITION OF THE PARCEL TO LIFT. FOR ITYPE=1 THE
36 ! PARCEL WITH THE WARMEST THETA-E IN A DPBND PASCAL LAYER ABOVE
37 ! THE MODEL SURFACE IS LIFTED. THE ARRAYS P1D, T1D, AND Q1D
38 ! ARE NOT USED. FOR ITYPE=2 THE ARRAYS P1D, T1D, AND Q1D
39 ! DEFINE THE PARCEL TO LIFT IN EACH COLUMN. BOTH TYPES OF
40 ! CAPE/CINS MAY BE COMPUTED IN A SINGLE EXECUTION OF THE POST
41 ! PROCESSOR.
42 !
43 ! THIS ALGORITHM PROCEEDS AS FOLLOWS.
44 ! FOR EACH COLUMN,
45 ! (1) INITIALIZE RUNNING CAPE AND CINS SUM TO 0.0
46 ! (2) COMPUTE TEMPERATURE AND PRESSURE AT THE LCL USING
47 ! LOOK UP TABLE (PTBL). USE EITHER PARCEL THAT GIVES
48 ! MAX THETAE IN LOWEST DPBND ABOVE GROUND (ITYPE=1)
49 ! OR GIVEN PARCEL FROM T1D,Q1D,...(ITYPE=2).
50 ! (3) COMPUTE THE TEMP OF A PARCEL LIFTED FROM THE LCL.
51 ! WE KNOW THAT THE PARCEL'S
52 ! EQUIVALENT POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE (THESP) REMAINS
53 ! CONSTANT THROUGH THIS PROCESS. WE CAN
54 ! COMPUTE TPAR USING THIS KNOWLEDGE USING LOOK
55 ! UP TABLE (SUBROUTINE TTBLEX).
56 ! (4) FIND THE EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL. THIS IS DEFINED AS THE
57 ! HIGHEST POSITIVELY BUOYANT LAYER.
58 ! (IF THERE IS NO POSITIVELY BUOYANT LAYER, CAPE/CINS
59 ! WILL BE ZERO)
60 ! (5) COMPUTE CAPE/CINS.
61 ! (A) COMPUTE THETAP. WE KNOW TPAR AND P.
62 ! (B) COMPUTE THETAA. WE KNOW T AND P.
63 ! (6) ADD G*(THETAP-THETAA)*DZ TO THE RUNNING CAPE OR CINS SUM.
64 ! (A) IF THETAP > THETAA, ADD TO THE CAPE SUM.
65 ! (B) IF THETAP < THETAA, ADD TO THE CINS SUM.
66 ! (7) ARE WE AT EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL?
67 ! (A) IF YES, STOP THE SUMMATION.
68 ! (B) IF NO, CONTIUNUE THE SUMMATION.
69 ! (8) ENFORCE LIMITS ON CAPE AND CINS (I.E. NO NEGATIVE CAPE)
70 !

74 ! TYPE 2 CAPE/CINS CALCULATIONS.
