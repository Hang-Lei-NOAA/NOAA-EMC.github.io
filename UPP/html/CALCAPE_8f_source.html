3 !$$$ SUBPROGRAM DOCUMENTATION BLOCK
4 ! . . .
5 ! SUBPROGRAM: CALCAPE COMPUTES CAPE AND CINS
6 ! PRGRMMR: TREADON ORG: W/NP2 DATE: 93-02-10
7 !
8 ! ABSTRACT:
9 !
10 ! THIS ROUTINE COMPUTES CAPE AND CINS GIVEN TEMPERATURE,
11 ! PRESSURE, AND SPECIFIC HUMIDTY. IN "STORM AND CLOUD
12 ! DYNAMICS" (1989, ACADEMIC PRESS) COTTON AND ANTHES DEFINE
13 ! CAPE (EQUATION 9.16, P501) AS
14 !
15 ! EL
16 ! CAPE = SUM G * LN(THETAP/THETAA) DZ
17 ! LCL
18 !
19 ! WHERE,
20 ! EL = EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL,
21 ! LCL = LIFTING CONDENSTATION LEVEL,
22 ! G = GRAVITATIONAL ACCELERATION,
23 ! THETAP = LIFTED PARCEL POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE,
24 ! THETAA = AMBIENT POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE.
25 !
26 ! NOTE THAT THE INTEGRAND LN(THETAP/THETAA) APPROXIMATELY
27 ! EQUALS (THETAP-THETAA)/THETAA. THIS RATIO IS OFTEN USED
28 ! IN THE DEFINITION OF CAPE/CINS.
29 !
30 ! TWO TYPES OF CAPE/CINS CAN BE COMPUTED BY THIS ROUTINE. THE
31 ! SUMMATION PROCESS IS THE SAME FOR BOTH CASES. WHAT DIFFERS
32 ! IS THE DEFINITION OF THE PARCEL TO LIFT. FOR ITYPE=1 THE
33 ! PARCEL WITH THE WARMEST THETA-E IN A DPBND PASCAL LAYER ABOVE
34 ! THE MODEL SURFACE IS LIFTED. THE ARRAYS P1D, T1D, AND Q1D
35 ! ARE NOT USED. FOR ITYPE=2 THE ARRAYS P1D, T1D, AND Q1D
36 ! DEFINE THE PARCEL TO LIFT IN EACH COLUMN. BOTH TYPES OF
37 ! CAPE/CINS MAY BE COMPUTED IN A SINGLE EXECUTION OF THE POST
38 ! PROCESSOR.
39 !
40 ! THIS ALGORITHM PROCEEDS AS FOLLOWS.
41 ! FOR EACH COLUMN,
42 ! (1) INITIALIZE RUNNING CAPE AND CINS SUM TO 0.0
43 ! (2) COMPUTE TEMPERATURE AND PRESSURE AT THE LCL USING
44 ! LOOK UP TABLE (PTBL). USE EITHER PARCEL THAT GIVES
45 ! MAX THETAE IN LOWEST DPBND ABOVE GROUND (ITYPE=1)
46 ! OR GIVEN PARCEL FROM T1D,Q1D,...(ITYPE=2).
47 ! (3) COMPUTE THE TEMP OF A PARCEL LIFTED FROM THE LCL.
48 ! WE KNOW THAT THE PARCEL'S
49 ! EQUIVALENT POTENTIAL TEMPERATURE (THESP) REMAINS
50 ! CONSTANT THROUGH THIS PROCESS. WE CAN
51 ! COMPUTE TPAR USING THIS KNOWLEDGE USING LOOK
52 ! UP TABLE (SUBROUTINE TTBLEX).
53 ! (4) FIND THE EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL. THIS IS DEFINED AS THE
54 ! HIGHEST POSITIVELY BUOYANT LAYER.
55 ! (IF THERE IS NO POSITIVELY BUOYANT LAYER, CAPE/CINS
56 ! WILL BE ZERO)
57 ! (5) COMPUTE CAPE/CINS.
58 ! (A) COMPUTE THETAP. WE KNOW TPAR AND P.
59 ! (B) COMPUTE THETAA. WE KNOW T AND P.
60 ! (6) ADD G*(THETAP-THETAA)*DZ TO THE RUNNING CAPE OR CINS SUM.
61 ! (A) IF THETAP > THETAA, ADD TO THE CAPE SUM.
62 ! (B) IF THETAP < THETAA, ADD TO THE CINS SUM.
63 ! (7) ARE WE AT EQUILIBRIUM LEVEL?
64 ! (A) IF YES, STOP THE SUMMATION.
65 ! (B) IF NO, CONTIUNUE THE SUMMATION.
66 ! (8) ENFORCE LIMITS ON CAPE AND CINS (I.E. NO NEGATIVE CAPE)
67 !
68 ! PROGRAM HISTORY LOG:
69 ! 93-02-10 RUSS TREADON
70 ! 93-06-19 RUSS TREADON - GENERALIZED ROUTINE TO ALLOW FOR
71 ! TYPE 2 CAPE/CINS CALCULATIONS.
72 ! 94-09-23 MIKE BALDWIN - MODIFIED TO USE LOOK UP TABLES
73 ! INSTEAD OF COMPLICATED EQUATIONS.
74 ! 94-10-13 MIKE BALDWIN - MODIFIED TO CONTINUE CAPE/CINS CALC

85 ! - ADDED EQ LVL HGHT AND THUNDER PARAMETER
